World of Tanks Community League

World of Tanks: Xbox was one of my favorite games before I moved to PC master race. I used to stream it on Twitch.tv before I came to FSU. I helped found the WoT Community Tournaments (WCT) and worked with the PR team at Wargaming (WoT's developers) for sponsorships and prizes for the events. I was one of three administrators for the event. Each event grew in size until we needed a proper league structure. This is when it evolved from WCT to WCL. I didn't think I would have time for administrating the events along with school so I left shortly before coming to FSU. In my last event we had over 110 teams participating from North America, South America, the United Kingdom, Russia, Australia, and Poland to name a few. Each team consisted of around 7-13 members. It became such a popular event in the community that we partnered with a company called Battlefy as a structural platform for the event.

Administration was an amazing experience regardless of all the issues with language barriers, time zones, and nation holidays that threw a wrench into the machine. It was worth every minute I spent helping out.
